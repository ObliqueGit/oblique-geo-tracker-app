# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Oblique agency staff (Sean + small team). Opens the tool in focused work sessions — before client calls, during weekly reporting, or while running audits. The primary job is to quickly read GEO visibility scores across clients and decide whether to act: run an audit, investigate a drop, prep a report. Speed and clarity matter more than anything else. No hand-holding needed; the users understand what GEO scores mean.

## Product Purpose

An internal AI visibility tracker for Oblique's client portfolio. Tracks how often each client's brand appears in responses from ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity across tracked prompts. Surfaces scores, deltas, platform breakdowns, and audit history. Helps the team diagnose visibility problems and demonstrate GEO value to clients. Success looks like: open the dashboard, see the state of all clients at a glance, act in under 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Confident. Calm.

Voice is direct and clinical — no hype, no filler. Labels name what the thing does. Numbers speak louder than adjectives. The tool should feel like something the team built for themselves: no marketing gloss, no unnecessary chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (DataDog, HubSpot, Salesforce) — blue-on-white, metric card grids, "Enterprise" aesthetic, dashboard theater.
- SEO tool UIs (Ahrefs, SEMrush) — orange/blue, information overload, aggressive upgrade CTAs.
- AI startup marketing dashboards — purple gradients, glassmorphism, glowing metrics, "next-gen" aesthetic, performance art over function.
- Notion/Coda — block-editor feel, overly document-like, not enough hierarchy for a data tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero.** Every layout decision should make scores and trends easier to read, not frame them decoratively.
2. **Earn chrome.** No borders, shadows, or containers that don't do structural work. If it doesn't separate, group, or anchor — remove it.
3. **Fast path first.** The primary action on any screen (run audit, view client, check score) should be reachable in one step. Secondary actions recede until needed.
4. **Internal confidence.** This is a tool the team shows clients. It should look like it was built by people who take design seriously — without ever looking like it was built to impress.
5. **Oblique, not neutral.** The tool has an owner. It should feel distinctly like Oblique's work, not a template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Color alone must never be the sole signal for score status (pair with label or icon alongside green/amber/red). Standard keyboard navigation throughout. No reduced-motion requirements flagged, but honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any transitions added.
